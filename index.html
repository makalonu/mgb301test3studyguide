MGB 301: Test 3 Study Guide (Spring 2026)

ORG. STRUCTURE

Organizational Structure: How job tasks are formally divided, grouped, and coordinated.

6 Key Elements of Structure

  • 1. Work Specialization: Degree to which tasks are subdivided into separate jobs. Pros: Efficient use of skills via repetition. Cons: Human diseconomies (boredom, fatigue, stress, high turnover) if taken too far.
  • 2. Departmentalization: Basis by which jobs are grouped.
    - Functional: Grouped by functions performed (efficiencies).
    - Product: Grouped by product line (increases accountability).
    - Geography: Grouped by territory (Best for scattered customers with localized needs).
    - Process/Customer: Grouped by product flow or specific client base.
  • 3. Chain of Command: Unbroken line of authority extending from top to bottom. Authority: Right of a manager to give orders. Unity of Command: Subordinates should have only ONE superior to whom they report.
  • 4. Span of Control: Number of subordinates a manager can direct. Wider: Cheaper, faster decisions, empowers workers. Narrow: Better control, but expensive and complicates vertical comms.
  • 5. Centralization: Decision-making concentrated at top. Decentralized: Decision-making pushed down to managers closest to the action. (Locos Tacos example). Results in faster action and higher morale.
  • 6. Formalization: Degree jobs are standardized. High: Strict rules. Low: High employee discretion.

Common Organizational Designs

  • Simple Structure: Low departmentalization, wide spans, centralized, little formalization. (Startups. Risk: Inadequate as org grows).
  • Bureaucracy (Weber): Routine operating tasks via specialization, formalized rules, centralized, narrow spans. (Pros: Efficient. Cons: Obsessive concern with rules, silos).
  • Matrix Structure: Combines Functional and Product departmentalization. Breaks Unity of Command (2 bosses). (Risk: Power struggles).

New Structural Options & Models

  • Virtual Structure: Small core org that outsources major business functions. Highly centralized, little/no departmentalization.
  • Boundaryless Org (Welch/GE): Eliminates chain of command, limitless spans of control, replaces departments with empowered teams.

Mechanistic Model: Synonymous with Bureaucracy. High specialization, rigid, clear chain, narrow spans, centralized, high formalization.

Organic Model: Synonymous with Boundaryless. Cross-functional teams, free flow of info, wide spans, decentralized, low formalization.

ORG. CULTURE

Culture: System of shared meaning held by members. It is a descriptive term (how employees perceive it). Job Satisfaction is an evaluative term.

Org vs National Culture: National culture has a GREATER impact on employees than their organization's culture.

7 Primary Characteristics

  1. Innovation & Risk Taking: Degree employees are encouraged to be innovative.
  2. Attention to Detail: Expectation of precision/analysis.
  3. Outcome Orientation: Focus on results/ends rather than techniques.
  4. People Orientation: Decisions factor in effect on people.
  5. Team Orientation: Work organized around teams.
  6. Aggressiveness: People are competitive vs easygoing.
  7. Stability: Emphasize maintaining status quo vs growth.

Types & Functions of Culture

  • Dominant Culture: Core values shared by majority.
  • Subcultures: Minicultures defined by dept/geography.
  • Strong Culture: Core values intensely held/widely shared. Acts as substitute for formalization; high behavioral control; reduces turnover.
  • Culture's Functions: 1) Boundary-defining. 2) Conveys identity. 3) Facilitates commitment. 4) Enhances social stability. 5) Sense-making/control mechanism.
  • Liability: Barrier to Change, Diversity, and M&A.

Creating & Sustaining Culture

Creation: Founders hire those who think like them, indoctrinate them, and act as role models.

How Employees Learn Culture:
- Stories: Narratives about founders, past mistakes.
- Rituals: Repetitive activities expressing core values.
- Material Symbols: Office size, perks, dress code.
- Language: Jargon/acronyms unique to the org.

The Socialization Model (3 Stages):
1. Prearrival: Learning that occurs before joining.
2. Encounter: New employee sees what org is really like (expectations clash with reality).
3. Metamorphosis: Employee adjusts to work and norms.

Conditions for Cultural Change: 1) Dramatic crisis exists. 2) Turnover in top leadership. 3) Org is young/small. 4) Existing culture is weak.

POWER & POLITICS

Power: Capacity A has to influence B to act in accordance with A's wishes. The key to power is DEPENDENCY.

General Dependency Postulate: The greater B's dependence on A, the more power A has. Dependency increases when resources are: Important, Scarce, Nonsubstitutable.

Leadership vs Power: Leadership requires goal congruence, downward influence. Power requires ONLY dependence, used in all directions.

Bases of Power (Formal vs Personal)

Formal Power: Based on position in org.

  • Coercive Power: Dependent on fear of negative results.
  • Reward Power: Ability to distribute desired rewards.
  • Legitimate Power: Formal authority to control resources.

Personal Power: Power stemming from unique characteristics. Most effective source of power.

  • Expert Power: Influence via expertise or special skill.
  • Referent Power: Based on identification with a person who has desirable resources or traits (e.g., charisma).

9 Influence Tactics (Yukl)

  1. Rational Persuasion: Logical arguments and factual evidence. The ONLY tactic effective across all levels (especially upward/boss).
  2. Inspirational Appeals: Appealing to values/hopes.
  3. Consultation: Involving target in deciding how to act.
  4. Ingratiation: Flattery/praise PRIOR to a request.
  5. Exchange: Rewarding target with benefits.
  6. Personal Appeals: Asking based on friendship.
  7. Coalitions: Enlisting aid of others to persuade.
  8. Legitimacy: Relying on your authority position.
  9. Pressure: Using warnings, demands, threats.

Politics in Organizations

Political Behavior: Activities not required by formal role, but influence distribution of advantages within org.

  • Legitimate: Normal politics (complaining, networking).
  • Illegitimate: Extreme behavior that violates implied rules (sabotage, whistle-blowing).

Causes of Political Behavior:
- Individual Factors: High self-monitors, internal locus of control, Machiavellianism, high need for power.
- Organizational Factors: Declining resources, promo opportunities, low trust, role ambiguity, zero-sum rewards.

ORGANIZATIONAL CHANGE

Forces for Change: Nature of workforce, Technology, Economic shocks, Competition, Social trends, World politics.

Change Agents: Persons who act as catalysts and manage change (managers, employees, or consultants).

Resistance to Change

  • Individual: Habit, Security, Economic factors, Fear of the unknown, Selective info processing.
  • Organizational: Structural inertia, Limited focus of change, Group inertia, Threat to expertise/power.

Overcoming Resistance

  • Education & Comms: Fight misinformation.
  • Participation: Hard to resist change you helped create.
  • Support & Commitment: Counseling, skills training.
  • Fairness: Implementing changes equitably.
  • Manipulation & Cooptation: Manipulation = twisting facts. Cooptation = manipulation + participation (buying off leaders of a resistance group to get endorsement).
  • Coercion: Direct threats/force (last resort).

Lewin's 3-Step Change Model

  1. Unfreezing: Overcoming status quo by increasing Driving Forces (direct behavior away from status quo) and decreasing Restraining Forces (hinder movement).
  2. Movement: Actual shift to new state.
  3. Refreezing: Stabilizing change by balancing driving and restraining forces so org doesn't revert.

Idea Champions: Individuals who actively promote an idea, build support, and ensure implementation. Traits: High self-confidence, persistence, energy, risk-taking.

RAPID RECALL (TEST HITS)

  • Rational Persuasion = Best for upward influence.
  • Geography Dept. = Best for scattered customers.
  • Work Specialization = Efficient use of skills via repetition.
  • Referent Power = Based on traits/charisma.
  • Ingratiation = Flattery BEFORE asking for a favor.
  • Matrix structures purposely break Unity of Command.
  • Substitution is NOT an influence tactic.
  • Cooptation combines manipulation and participation.
  • Locos Tacos Example: Pushing decision-making down to store managers = Decentralization.
  • Mechanistic = Bureaucracy. Organic = Boundaryless.
